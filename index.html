Pot-Cover Effect Experiment — Project Index

Research Objective

“Canopy effect” of “Pot-Cover effect” refers to ascending vapor condensation near a barrier and lead to water accumulation near the barrier.

In geotechnical engineering, some pavements in cold regions with relatively dry climate and low water tables still suffer from weakened base and subgrade due to water accumulation. The cause of such problem could be this “Canopy effect”, as the vapor condense under the asphalt layer, wetting the soils under the layer.

This water accumulation with poor drainage could weaken the soil. During freezing season, the water aids the formation of ice lenses in soil, thus could induce significant frost heaves to the road or runway.

This research aims to investigate a wicking nonwoven geotextile’s efficacy in addressing this issue by actively draining water out of unsaturated soil, thus mitigating the water accumulation and frost heave problem.

This proposed lab test would provide valuable insight into the canopy effect in unsaturated coarse-grained soil, as well as the efficacy of the wicking nonwoven geotextile in managing the vapor induced moisture. Hopefully this test would quantify the benefits of a wicking nonwoven geotextile for mitigating the canopy effect.
